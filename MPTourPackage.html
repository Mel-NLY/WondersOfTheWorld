Cusco to Machu Picchu Day Trip

From $347   Rating: 4.5/5
Route and Destinations: Ollantaytambo - Aguas Calientes - Machu Picchu - Cusco

Take an all-inclusive day trip to breathtaking Machu Picchu from Cusco.
Visit the world-famous Incan ruins and learn about the city, its history and the people who once lived there.
Ideal for first-time visitors, families and those travelling solo, this once-in-a-lifetime experience is a must-do when visiting Peru!

Brief Itinerary
Day Time Activity Further Description
Day 1 8.00 - 9.30 Travel to Ollantaytambo train station 1.5 hours away from Cusco.
There you would board the Executive Class train and make your way to Aguas Calientes station in comfort. 		Unfortunately, dinner will not be provided.
9.30 - 12.00 Enjoy scenic panoramic views from the train, where you'll be seated in comfortable, padded chairs with a fold-out table for your convenience for the 2 1/2 hour journey.
Snacks and beverages are served on board the train for your enjoyment (at own expense).
12.00 - 13.30 Meet your tour guide over lunch and experience Peruvian cuisine in the town of Aguas Calientes.
You would then head off for Machu Picchu after lunch.
13.30 - 18.00 Your expert guide will walk with you through the main streets of these amazing ruins.
Stroll through town squares and climb delicately carved stone stairways.
There will be time allocated where your guide leaves you alone to fully embrace this magnificent wonder of the world and let this once-in-a-lifetime experience set in.
18.00 - 22.00 It is time to return back to Cusco. You would then bid farewell to your guide and return back via train again.

Inca Trail Short 2-Day Trek to Machu Picchu

From $688   Rating: 5/5
Route and Destinations: Wiñay Wayna - Intipunku - Aguas Calientes - Machu Picchu - Huayna Picchu - Cusco

Along this trail, you would passby many sites of significance such as
Wiñay Wayna (translates to Forever Young), the most impressive site on the Incan Trail and
Initpunku (Sun Gate), which held great religious significance to the Incans,
before stopping at the nearby town of Aguas Calientes to rest before arriving at Machu Picchu.

Brief Itinerary
Day Time Activity Further Description
Day 1 4.00 - 12.00 You would be picked up from the hotel by one of our private cars and driven for around 1 1/2 hours to the train station.
You'll travel on a very picturesque route for two and a half hours via train to the starting point of the trek (Kilometer 104).
You'll then have a trek of approximately 4 hours up to the Inca site of Wiñay Wayna, the most impressive site on the Inca Trail. 		Trekkers are required to pack their own lunch; breakfast would be provided in the train.
12.00 - 13.30 You would have lunch at Wiñay Wayna, admiring the picturesque view and archaelogical structures of the Incan ruin.
13.30 - 17.00 After lunch, you will continue on to Intipunku, the Sun Gate, the entrance to Machu Picchu.
This is where you would have your first breathtaking, panoramic view of Machu Picchu.
17.00 - 20.00 As you are about to reach Machu Picchu, you would take a side path down into the nearby town of Aguas Calientes.
You will eat dinner there, interact with the native Peruvians and also rest for the night before Day 2.
Day 2 6.00 - 7.00 Start off the day with your breakfast before taking the first bus to Machu Picchu. Lunch is not included in the cost of the tour package.
An extra night in Aguas Calientes can also be arranged to explore Machu Picchu for another day.
If you desire, you could also visit the thermal springs in Aguas Calientes - a treat to yourself for completing the trail.
7.00 - 12.30 Your tour guide would bring you on a walking tour of approximately 2 hours and after that you can explore Machu Picchu on your own.
If you still have enough energy to continue trekking, you can purchase a ticket to climb Huayna Picchu.
It takes about 45 minutes to reach the peak, where you can get a magnificent view of Machu Picchu and its surroundings.
You will later meet your guide back in Aguas Calientes for lunch and to receive your train tickets back to Cusco.

Huchuy Qosqo Trek to Machu Picchu 3 Days - 2 Nights

From $600   Rating: 4.5/5
Route and Destinations: Patamba - Pucamarca - Huchuyqosqo - Chinchero - Ollantaytambo - Aguas Calientes - Macchu Picchu - Huayna Picchu - Cusco

This trek is ideal for those who like to be in the middle of nowhere with only nature surrounding them.
The Huchuy Qosqo trek allows you to camp in an area that is seldom seen by tourists.
Huchuy Qosqo (small Cusco) is situated above the Urubamba Valley and offers spectacular views over the Cordillera de Vilcanota mountain range.
This trek is also a great way for you to enjoy a shorter trek that still includes Machu Picchu and the Inca trails (with stairs more preseved than the main Inca Trail).
You will have one night of camping in the wilderness and one night in a hostel in Aguas Calientes.

Brief Itinerary
Day Time Activity Further Description
Day 1 6.00 - 9.00 Early morning you will be picked up from your hotel and transferred to the small village of Patabamba
where wranglers and a group of llamas and mules are waiting. 		You would have to pack your own lunch.
9.00 - 12.30 After loading the llamas and mules, you will start your walk through the high valleys to Pucamarca.
Along the way you will have fantastic views of the surrounding snow capped mountains some high mountain lakes.
12.30 - 14.00 After reaching Pucamarca, you would settle down and eat your lunch, before resuming on your journey.
14.00 - 20.00 While on your journey past Pucamarca, you would passby more Incan monuments before reaching Huchuyqosqo.
The wranglers would then start setting up tents and preparing dinner to stay overnight at the camp.
Day 2 7.00 - 9.00 After having your breakfast at your camp, your wranglers would give a guided visit of Huchuyqosqo before continuing on your hike. -
9.00 - 13.30 After the tour of Huchuy Qosqo, you would trek for about 3 hours to the small town of Chinchero.
You would visit a local home, where there would be traditional weaving demonstrations and you would have your lunch.
13.30 - 18.00 After leaving Chinchero, your private transport would drive you to Ollantaytambo,
where you would catch the afternoon train to Aguas Calientes.
After arrival at Aguas Calientes, you would check into your hotel room, have dinner and rest up for the next day.
Day 3 6.00 - 7.00 Start off the day with your breakfast before taking the first bus to Machu Picchu.
7.00 - 12.30 Your tour guide would bring you on a walking tour of approximately 2 hours and after that you can explore Machu Picchu on your own.
If you still have enough energy to continue trekking, you can purchase a ticket to climb Huayna Picchu.
It takes about 45 minutes to reach the peak, where you can get a magnificent view of Machu Picchu and its surroundings.
You will later meet your guide back in Aguas Calientes for one last lunch and to receive your train tickets back to Cusco.
